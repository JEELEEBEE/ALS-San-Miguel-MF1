What is Alternative Learning System - ALS?

Alternative Learning System or ALS is a program designed to provide non-formal, accessible education to individuals who are out of school or cannot attend traditional school settings. It aims to help learners develop basic literacy, numeracy, and life skills, as well as technical and vocational skills, to improve their personal, social, and economic well-being. ALS is often used to reach out to out-of-school youth, adults, and marginalized groups, offering flexible learning options to promote lifelong learning and inclusive education.

Purpose and Significance

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is designed to provide non-formal education to out-of-school youth, adults, and marginalized groups who cannot access or complete formal schooling. Its purpose is to help learners develop basic literacy, numeracy, life skills, and vocational skills to improve their personal lives, employability, and social participation. Its significance lies in promoting inclusive education, ensuring that no one is left behind, and empowering individuals to contribute positively to their communities and the economy. ALS helps bridge educational gaps, fosters lifelong learning, and supports social equity by reaching learners in diverse settings and circumstances.

Discovering Alternative Learning System opens the door to lifelong learning, empowering every Filipino to grow, learn, and succeed beyond the classroom.
